Summary: Where We Landed

Scope

Our goal was to determine production trends for green and conventional energy sources. We were also curious to see whether political ideology influenced growth in green energy.

Methodology

We examined data from the U.S. Energy Information Association detailing energy sources by state from 1990-2018 in the United States. To examine the impact of political ideology on energy production, we used annual state ideology scores from William D. Berry, Evan J. Ringquist, Richard C. Fording, Russell L. Hanson as published in the Journal of Political Science in 1998, which provides ideology measures from 1960 and has been maintained through 2017.

Energy sources were categorized as green or conventional, then used as input for regression-based machine learning models to determine potential trends in green and conventional energy production.

To determine the impact of ideology, we merged Fording et al’s ideology score with the dataset and calculated additional information related to change in green vs conventional energy production.

Results

The data indicate that in 1990 green energy production in the U.S stood at 1.7 billion megawatt hours (Mwh) and energy produced by conventional sources was 4.3 billion (Mwh). Total green energy production in the U.S. has risen from 29% of energy produced in 1990 to 34% in 2018. Among green energy sources, wind and hydroelectric accounted for nearly 40% of green energy produced in 2018. Energy production from wind farms has steadily increased since 1990, accounting for less than 1% of green energy to 20% in 2018. The biggest gains have been in solar energy production, with solar sources contributing 1.2 million Mwh as recently as 2010 and 63.8 million in 2018.

Next Steps

Although ideology did not prove to be a good predictor of energy trends, there are a variety of other demographics that likely impact green energy production. Some are present in the prepared database, such as population and median income. Exploration of these variables' impact may prove fruitful.
